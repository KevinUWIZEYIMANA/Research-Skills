Course Summary

Research Skills is a comprehensive course designed to enhance your research capabilities. This course covers a wide range of topics, including nature of research, Research in Project Management, Overview of the research proposal, Background to the study and problem statement, Research objectives, questions and hypotheses and other topics that can help an individual to make a good research proposal. The course emphasizes practical applications and critical thinking skills, preparing you for real-world research challenges.

Topics to covers

  • Introduction to academic research
  • The nature of research
  • Research in Project Management
  • Overview of the research proposal
  • Research topic formulation
  • Background to the study and problem statement
  • Research objectives, questions and hypotheses
  • Justification of the study and theoretical framework
  • Introduction to literature review and referencing styles
  • Methodology: target population, sample size and ethical considerations
  • Methodology: research approach and research design
  • Methodology: methods, tools for data collection and data analysis
  • From research proposal to research report
  • Research data processing and analysis
  • Research data presentation and discussion
  • Develop research conclusion and recommendations

Why study Research Skills as Project Manager?

As a Project Manager, studying research skills is crucial because it enables informed decision-making, ensures that project goals are based on evidence, and strengthens communication with stakeholders. This course has provided the foundation to explore problems systematically, analyze relevant data, and apply results to improve project planning and execution. Strong research abilities not only enhance the quality of outcomes but also contribute to reducing risks and optimizing resources.

Key reasons why research skills are important for a Project Manager:

  • Informed Decision-Making: Enables data-driven choices rather than relying on assumptions.
  • Accurate Needs Assessment: Helps understand client or stakeholder requirements clearly.
  • Problem-Solving: Supports identifying root causes and formulating effective solutions.
  • Project Planning: Assists in realistic scheduling, budgeting, and resource allocation.
  • Effective Communication: Provides evidence to support proposals and gain stakeholder trust.
  • Risk Management: Helps anticipate challenges and plan mitigation strategies.
  • Tool Proficiency: Enhances ability to use research tools like surveys, reports, and analytics platforms.
